Conclusion on self-determination and university presses here-- why is it important that there is agency in deciding who controls the publishing, dissemination of their own outputs?
Publicly-funded knowledge is a common good, and researchers and publishers have a responsibility to the public to provide free access to these research products \cite{logan2017we}. Additionally, researchers have a responsibility to the public to conduct rigorous research that provides the best value for money, which will, in turn, earn public trust. Therefore, it is crucial that researchers retain control over how their research is conducted and disseminated. If researchers retain such control, they can act in the best interests of academia whose goal is to share knowledge, rather than in the best interests of publishers whose goal is to maximize profits \cite{Logan2017}.
Scholarly publishing and academic self-determination are interdependent and a crucial point of future debate for the future of University presses and Open Access worldwide.
