Through this output the authors, members of international academic communities specialising in scholarly communications, document publicly the agreement betweent
On February 1, 2015,  the international corporation Elsevier and the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) established the agreement UNAM-Elsevier contract DGAJ-DPI-39-081114-241, which saw the transfer of  "production and hosting, advertising and support " of 44 Mexican open access academic journals published by UNAM to Elsevier. 
We describe a Freedom of Information Request enquiring the total cost of the contract and its corresponding response, and share a series of recommendations that, based on this case, we argue can be helpful to other institutions that may face similar circumstances in the future. We conclude scholarly publishing and academic self-determination are interdependent and a crucial point of future debate for the future of University presses and Open Access worldwide.
