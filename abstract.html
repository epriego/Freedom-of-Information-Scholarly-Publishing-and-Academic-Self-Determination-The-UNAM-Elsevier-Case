As members of international academic communities specialising in scholarly communications, the authors wish to document publicly the agreement between transnational corporation Elsevier and the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) established on February 1, 2015. The agreement, under the UNAM-Elsevier contract DGAJ-DPI-39-081114-241, saw the transfer of  "Production and accommodation, advertising and support "of 44 academic journals of open access of the UNAM to international publishing company Elsevier. We describe a Freedom of Information Request enquiring the total cost of the contract and its corresponding response, and share a series of recommendations that, based on this case, we believe can be helpful to other institutions that may face similar circumstances in the future.
