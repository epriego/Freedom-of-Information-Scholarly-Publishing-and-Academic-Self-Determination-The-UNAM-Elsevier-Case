Introduction

The intention of this output is to document and discuss publicly the agreement between transnational corporation Elsevier and the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM)  signed on 1 February 2015.
The agreement, UNAM-Elsevier contract DGAJ-DPI-39-081114-241, saw the transfer of  "production and hosting, advertising and support "of 44 UNAM-published academic open access journals to publishing company Elsevier \cite{ernesto2017} \cite{ernesto2017a}.  A redacted PDF of a scanned copy of the contract document was leaked anonymously and made available on-line through an anonymous blog post \cite{unam}. We became aware of the contract doing on-line searches related to Elsevier.
The main body of this output has been adapted from the text that was collectively discussed and co-authored online as a potential 'open letter to the international academic community' over a period of three months in direct collaboration with colleagues from UNAM who unfortunately, for different reasons, felt unable to sign publicly. That original letter was signed by  25 international academics from 9 different countries including Mexico. 
We respect our colleagues' desire for anonymity and understand the pressures they are under. We are also fully aware that we who sign this output are mostly academics based in institutions in the Global North and therefore not necessarily implicated in or directly affected by the contract in question.
